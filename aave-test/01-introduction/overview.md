# Aave Protocol Overview

Aave is a decentralized non-custodial liquidity market protocol where users can participate as suppliers or borrowers. Suppliers provide liquidity to the market to earn a passive income, while borrowers are able to borrow in an overcollateralized (perpetually) or undercollateralized (one-block liquidity) fashion.

## Key Versions

### Aave V3 (Current Mainnet Standard)
Launched in 2022, V3 focused on capital efficiency and risk management.
*   **Key Features:** Portals (cross-chain), Efficiency Mode (E-Mode), Isolation Mode, and Siloed Borrowing.
*   **Deployment:** Live on Ethereum, Arbitrum, Optimism, Polygon, Avalanche, Base, and more.

### Aave V4 (In Development)
Announced in 2024, V4 proposes a modular "Hub-and-Spoke" architecture.
*   **Key Features:** Unified Liquidity Layer, custom lending modules (Spokes), and a fully new codebase designed for the next decade of DeFi.
*   **Timeline:** Expected late 2025 / 2026.

## Governance
The protocol is governed by the **Aave DAO** and `AAVE` token holders. Governance decides:
*   **Risk Parameters:** LTV, Liquidation Thresholds, Supply Caps.
*   **Asset Listings:** Which tokens are accepted.
*   **Service Providers:** Hiring teams (Chaos Labs, ACI, etc.) to manage the protocol's operations.

## Developer Ecosystem
Aave provides a rich set of tools for developers:
*   **aave.js:** SDK for interacting with the protocol.
*   **Governance Utilities:** Tools for submitting proposals.
*   **Data:** Subgraphs and Indexers for historical data.
